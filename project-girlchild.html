Keep the girl child in school

Often, the cost of sanitary products for girls is simply too high, forcing them to skip school. In some countries, like Malawi, sanitary pads can cost the equivalent of an entire dayâ€™s salary. In India, it has been estimated that as many as 1 in 5 girls drop out of school after they get their periods. In some regions, like Maharashtra, that number is nearer to 4 in 5.

When a girl finishes secondary school, she is less likely to experience child marriage, face domestic abuse, and suffer from long-term health complications. As a result, educated women and girls are more likely to have fewer, healthier children. These children then, in turn, more likely to get an education and pull themselves out of poverty.
